Timeline of Major Breaches

  1. 2013 - Target: Hackers accessed the network through a third-party HVAC vendor, stealing 40 million credit and debit card numbers. (Source)
  2. 2014 - Sony Pictures: A destructive attack by state-sponsored actors that leaked unreleased films and embarrassed executives. (Source)
  3. 2017 - WannaCry: A huge ransomware attack that stopped hospitals and businesses worldwide using the EternalBlue exploit. (Source)
  4. 2017 - Equifax: Failure to patch a known vulnerability exposed the personal financial data (SSNs) of 147 million people. (Source)
  5. 2020 - SolarWinds: A supply chain attack where hackers compromised a software update to spy on US government agencies. (Source)
  6. 2021 - Colonial Pipeline: A ransomware attack that shut down a major US gas pipeline, causing fuel shortages. (Source)
  7. 2022 - Uber: Attackers used "MFA Fatigue" (spamming notifications) to annoy an employee into approving a login request. (Source)
  8. 2023 - MGM Resorts: A social engineering attack on the IT helpdesk that cost the casino giant over $100 million. (Source)
  9. 2024 - Change Healthcare: A massive ransomware breach impacting prescription processing and healthcare payments nationwide. (Source)
  10. 2024 - AT&T: Data for nearly all cellular customers was stolen after attackers accessed a third-party cloud storage workspace. (Source)

Types of Threats

  • APT Groups: State-sponsored hackers who conduct long-term espionage campaigns.
  • Ransomware: Malicious software that locks files and demands payment for the key.
  • Insider Threats: Current or former employees who misuse access to harm the organization.
  • Botnets: Networks of infected devices controlled remotely to launch attacks.
  • Phishing: Deceptive emails or messages designed to trick users into revealing sensitive info.
  • DDoS Attacks: Flooding a website or server with traffic to knock it offline.
  • Zero-Day Exploits: Attacks targeting software vulnerabilities that the vendor doesn't know about yet.
  • Man-in-the-Middle (MitM): Attackers secretly intercepting and relaying communication between two parties.
